Staff Reference Guide

SAGE EMR Tutorial

A step-by-step guide to every part of the system — from registering your first patient to generating monthly returns. Find your role below and follow along.

Who uses what?
RoleKey SectionsDaily Starting Point
Receptionist Patients, Scheduling, Billing Dashboard → Today's Queue
Nurse Queue, Triage, Encounters (vitals), Admissions (eMAR) Scheduling → Queue
Doctor / Resident Encounters, Prescriptions, Lab orders, Admissions Dashboard → Clinic Queue
Lab Technician Laboratory Worklist Laboratory → Worklist
Pharmacist Pharmacy Queue, Stock, Goods Receipt Pharmacy → Dashboard
Billing Officer Billing, Reports Billing → Invoice List

Part 1 — Getting Started

Logging In

All Staff
  1. Open your web browser and go to the SAGE EMR address provided by your IT department.
  2. Enter your email address and password.
  3. Click Sign In.
  4. If your account is protected by two-factor authentication, enter the 6-digit code from your authenticator app.
After 5 failed login attempts your account is locked for 30 minutes. Contact your administrator if this happens.
Your session will time out after a period of inactivity. Save your work and log back in if you see the login screen unexpectedly.

The Dashboard

All Staff

The Dashboard is the first screen you see after logging in. It gives you a live snapshot of the hospital right now.

  • Today's Patients — new registrations this morning.
  • Active Admissions — inpatients currently in beds.
  • Queue Today — patients waiting or in consultation.
  • Pending Labs — lab orders not yet released.

Below the stats, Today's Clinic Queue shows every patient booked for today. Click a patient's row to open their profile. Use the Waiting / In Progress / Done filter chips to focus on a specific group.

The Recent Activity panel on the right shows the latest events across the hospital in real time.

Part 2 — Reception & Registration

For Receptionists & Records Officers
Your job is to get patients into the system quickly and accurately. Every clinical interaction begins here.

Finding a Patient

Reception

Before registering anyone, always search first to avoid duplicate records.

  1. Click Patients in the left sidebar.
  2. Type the patient's name, hospital number, phone number, or NHIA ID in the search box. Results appear as you type.
  3. Click the patient's name to open their profile.

Use the filter chips at the top of the list — Inpatient shows only admitted patients, A&E shows today's emergency attendees.

Registering a New Patient

Reception
  1. Go to Patients → Register Patient (or click the button on the dashboard).
  2. Fill in the patient's details. Fields marked with a red * are required.
  3. Payer Type is important — select the correct one:
    • NHIA — National Health Insurance. Enter the NHIA number.
    • Private HMO — Enter the HMO name and member number.
    • Corporate / Employer — Company pays.
    • Self Pay — Patient pays directly.
  4. If the system detects a possible duplicate, it will show a warning. Review the listed patients carefully before continuing.
  5. Add Next of Kin details at the bottom.
  6. Click Register Patient. A unique hospital number (SAGE/YYYY/XXXXXX) is automatically generated.
Never ignore the duplicate warning. Two records for the same patient leads to split clinical history. Always check before creating a new record.

Booking an Appointment

Reception
  1. Go to Scheduling → Book Appointment.
  2. Search for and select the patient.
  3. Choose the Clinic (e.g., General Outpatient, Cardiology, ANC).
  4. Select a Date and available Time Slot.
  5. Set the Visit Type: New Patient, Follow-up, Procedure, or Review.
  6. Add a brief Reason for Visit (optional but helpful for the doctor).
  7. Click Book Appointment.

Walk-In Patients & Check-In

Reception

Walk-In (no prior appointment)

  1. Go to Scheduling → Walk-In.
  2. Search for the patient or register them if new.
  3. Select the clinic and add the patient to today's queue.

Checking In (patient with an appointment)

  1. Go to Scheduling → Appointments or the daily queue.
  2. Find the patient's appointment.
  3. Click Check In. This marks them as arrived and moves them to the active queue.

Part 3 — Nursing & Triage

For Nurses
You manage patient flow through the clinic — triage, vital signs, and ensuring the queue moves smoothly.

The Clinic Queue

Nurse

The queue is your central screen. Go to Scheduling → Queue.

  • Patients are listed in arrival order.
  • The coloured bar on the left of each row is the triage priority: ■ Red (immediate), ■ Amber (urgent), ■ Green (non-urgent).
  • Filter by Waiting, In Progress, or Done using the chips at the top.

Triaging a Patient

Nurse
  1. Find the patient in the queue and click their row.
  2. Click Triage.
  3. Assign a Priority Level:
    • 🔴 Red — Immediate: Life-threatening. Resuscitation required now.
    • 🟡 Amber — Urgent: Serious condition. See within 30 minutes.
    • 🟢 Green — Non-Urgent: Stable. Can wait for routine consultation.
  4. Add any triage notes (e.g., "Patient looks pale, complaining of chest pain").
  5. Save. The queue will re-order accordingly.
Vitals are recorded inside the encounter workspace. A doctor or nurse can enter them once the encounter is opened.

Part 4 — Clinical Workflow (Doctors & Residents)

For Doctors & Residents
The encounter is your digital consultation note. Everything — diagnoses, prescriptions, lab orders — flows from here.

Starting a Consultation (Encounter)

Doctor
  1. From the Dashboard or Queue, click the patient's name to open their profile.
  2. Click Start Encounter on the patient's profile page.
  3. Select the Encounter Type: OPD, A&E, IPD Ward Round, Procedure, or Telemedicine.
  4. The encounter workspace opens. You are now the attending clinician on record.
Starting an encounter automatically moves the patient's queue status from Waiting to With Doctor and generates a consultation invoice.

Writing SOAP Notes

Doctor

The encounter workspace is organised using the standard SOAP format.

SectionWhat to write
S — SubjectiveChief Complaint (why they came) and History of Presenting Illness (onset, duration, character, associated symptoms).
O — ObjectiveReview of Systems and Examination Findings (what you found on examination).
A — AssessmentYour clinical Assessment — working diagnosis and reasoning.
P — PlanManagement Plan — what you are going to do: investigations, medications, referrals, follow-up.

Notes auto-save as you type. You do not need to click a save button.

Recording Vital Signs

Nurse Doctor
  1. In the encounter workspace, find the Vitals section.
  2. Enter the readings: Temperature, Blood Pressure (systolic / diastolic), Pulse, SpO₂, Respiratory Rate, Weight, Height, Pain Score.
  3. Click Save Vitals.

Abnormal values are automatically highlighted in red — blood pressure above 140/90, pulse outside 60–100, temperature above 37.5 °C, or SpO₂ below 95%.

Adding Diagnoses

Doctor
  1. In the Diagnoses section of the encounter, click Add Diagnosis.
  2. Enter the Description (e.g., "Essential Hypertension").
  3. Optionally add the ICD-10 code for precision (e.g., I10).
  4. Set the type: Primary, Secondary, or Working/Provisional.
  5. Click Add.

You can add multiple diagnoses per encounter. Primary diagnoses appear first.

Prescribing Medication

Doctor
  1. In the Prescriptions section of the encounter, type the drug name to search the formulary.
  2. Select the correct drug from the dropdown (generic name and strength are shown).
  3. Fill in: Dose, Frequency, Route, Duration (days), and Quantity.
  4. Add any special Instructions for the patient.
  5. Click Add Prescription.
Safety Checks — Pay Attention:
  • Allergy Alert: If the drug matches a recorded allergy, a red warning appears. You must document a reason to override.
  • Drug Interaction: If there is a known interaction with another active prescription, an amber warning appears. Review and acknowledge before proceeding.

Frequency codes:

OD — Once daily   BD — Twice daily   TDS — Three times daily   QID — Four times daily   PRN — As needed   STAT — Immediately, once

Ordering Lab Tests

Doctor
  1. In the Lab Orders section of the encounter, search for the test by name or panel.
  2. Select the test (e.g., FBC, LFT, Malaria RDT).
  3. Set the Priority: Routine, Urgent, or STAT.
  4. Add Clinical Notes for the lab (optional but useful for unusual requests).
  5. Click Order Test.
Set priority to STAT for critical patients — this moves the order to the top of the lab worklist immediately.

Signing Off an Encounter

Doctor

When you are finished with the consultation, you must sign the encounter. This is the equivalent of completing and filing the patient's notes.

  1. Review all sections — SOAP notes, vitals, diagnoses, prescriptions, and lab orders.
  2. Click Sign & Close at the top of the workspace.
  3. Confirm in the dialog that appears.
Signing is permanent. Once signed, the encounter is locked and cannot be edited. Make sure all information is correct before signing.

After signing, the patient's queue status changes to Done, and their prescriptions become visible in the pharmacy queue.

Admitting a Patient

Doctor
  1. Open the patient's profile.
  2. Click Admit Patient.
  3. Select the Ward and an Available Bed from the bed map.
  4. Enter the Admitting Diagnosis and any admission notes.
  5. Confirm. The patient is now an inpatient — their bed shows as occupied on the ward map.

Ward Rounds & Medication Administration (eMAR)

Nurse Doctor

Adding a Ward Round Note

  1. Go to Admissions and click the patient.
  2. Click Add Round Note.
  3. Document the patient's current status, any changes to the plan, and your name.

Medication Administration Record (eMAR)

  1. In the patient's admission detail, find the eMAR section.
  2. Click the medication that was due.
  3. Set the outcome: Given, Held, Refused by Patient, or Not Available.
  4. Note the actual dose if it differed, and add any administration notes.
  5. Save.

Discharging a Patient

Doctor
  1. Go to Admissions, open the patient's admission record.
  2. Click Discharge.
  3. Select the Discharge Type: Routine, Self-discharge (Against Medical Advice), Transferred Out, or Deceased.
  4. Write a Discharge Summary — this is the clinical document sent with the patient.
  5. Confirm. The bed is released and shown as available on the ward map.

Booking a Surgery

Doctor
  1. Go to Operating Theatre in the sidebar.
  2. Click Book Surgery.
  3. Select the patient, theatre, lead surgeon, and anaesthetist.
  4. Enter the procedure name, scheduled date/time, and estimated duration.
  5. Set the priority: Elective, Urgent, or Emergency.
  6. Add pre-operative notes and assign team members if needed.
  7. Save. The booking status will show as Scheduled.
Scheduled→ Confirmed→ In Progress→ Completed

Part 5 — Laboratory

For Lab Technicians & Scientists
Every test goes through a defined workflow — from order to verified result. Follow each step carefully.
Ordered→ Sample Collected→ In Progress→ Resulted→ Verified→ Released

The Lab Worklist

Lab Tech

Go to Laboratory → Worklist. This is your main screen for the day.

  • Orders are sorted by priority: STAT first, then Urgent, then Routine.
  • Filter by panel (Haematology, Chemistry, Microbiology, etc.).
  • Each row shows the patient, test name, time ordered, and current status.
  • A progress bar shows where each order is in the workflow.

Collecting Samples

Lab Tech
  1. Find the order in the worklist (status: Ordered).
  2. Click the order to open it.
  3. Click Mark Sample Collected.
  4. The timestamp and your name are recorded automatically.

Entering Results

Lab Tech
  1. Open the order (status: Sample Collected or In Progress).
  2. Click Enter Results.
  3. Type the Value and confirm the Unit.
  4. Set the Flag: Normal, Low, High, Critical Low, Critical High, or Qualitative Abnormal.
  5. Save. Status moves to Resulted.

Verifying & Releasing Results

Lab Tech
  1. Open a resulted order.
  2. Review the value and flag for accuracy.
  3. Click Verify & Release.
  4. The result is now visible to the requesting clinician and in the patient's record.

Critical Values

Lab Tech
A Critical Value requires immediate action. When you flag a result as Critical Low or Critical High, the system automatically sends a notification to the requesting clinician.
  • The result will appear in red on the clinician's screen.
  • The clinician must click Acknowledge to confirm they have seen it.
  • Do not wait for acknowledgement — also call the clinician directly for life-threatening values.

Part 6 — Pharmacy

For Pharmacists
Your queue fills automatically as doctors sign encounters. Dispense in order, track stock, and flag any shortages.

Dispensing a Prescription

Pharmacist
  1. Go to Pharmacy. The dashboard shows today's pending prescriptions.
  2. Click Dispense next to a prescription.
  3. The system suggests the best batch to use (FEFO — First Expiry, First Out). Confirm or select a different batch.
  4. Enter the Quantity Dispensed if different from prescribed.
  5. Click Confirm Dispense. Stock is deducted automatically.
FEFO ensures older stock is used first, reducing waste from expired drugs.

Checking Stock Levels

Pharmacist

Go to Pharmacy → Stock.

  • Drugs highlighted in red are at or below the reorder level — action required.
  • Drugs highlighted in amber have a batch expiring within 90 days.
  • Click a drug to see all batches, quantities, and expiry dates.

Receiving New Stock (Goods Receipt)

Pharmacist
  1. Go to Pharmacy → Goods Receipt.
  2. Select the drug from the formulary.
  3. Enter the Batch Number, Expiry Date, Quantity Received, Unit Cost, and Supplier.
  4. Click Receive. Stock is added to inventory immediately.

Part 7 — Billing

For Billing Officers
Invoices are created automatically as clinical services are used. Your job is to collect payment and reconcile.

Finding and Reading an Invoice

Billing
  1. Go to Billing in the sidebar.
  2. Search by patient name or hospital number, or filter by status (Draft, Issued, Partial, Paid).
  3. Click an invoice to open it.

The invoice shows:

  • Line Items — each service rendered (consultation, lab test, drugs, bed fees).
  • Subtotal, any Discount, and the final Total.
  • Amount Paid and Balance Due.
  • Payment History — every payment received, with mode and reference.
Draft→ Issued→ Partial→ Paid

Recording a Payment

Billing
  1. Open the invoice.
  2. Click Record Payment.
  3. Enter the Amount.
  4. Select the Payment Mode: Cash, POS/Card, Bank Transfer, NHIA, or Waiver.
  5. Add a Reference Number (e.g., POS receipt, bank transfer reference).
  6. Click Save Payment. The balance updates automatically. If the balance reaches zero, the invoice status changes to Paid.
For online payments, click Pay via Paystack on the invoice. The patient or cashier is redirected to the Paystack secure page. Payment confirmation happens automatically via webhook.

Part 8 — Administration & Reports

For Hospital Administrators & Managers
Dashboards, regulatory returns, and claims processing.

Reading the Dashboards

Go to Reports in the sidebar. Three dashboards are available:

  • Operational — live bed occupancy, today's patient volume, queue stats, admissions and discharges.
  • Financial — revenue collected, outstanding receivables, payment mode breakdown.
  • Clinical — encounter types, top diagnoses, lab trends, dispensing statistics.

Monthly Return & NHIA Claims

Billing

Monthly Return

  1. Go to Reports → Monthly Return.
  2. Select the Year and Month from the dropdowns.
  3. The NHMIS statistics are compiled automatically from patient and encounter records.
  4. Review and submit.

NHIA Claims

  1. Go to Reports → NHIA Claims.
  2. Click New Claim Batch.
  3. Add the relevant invoices to the batch.
  4. Click Submit Batch to send to the insurer.
  5. Track claim status (Submitted → Queried → Approved → Paid).

Part 9 — Patient Portal

For Patients
Patients can view their appointments, results, and bills from their phone — no need to come to the counter for routine enquiries.

Direct patients to the Patient Portal link. They log in using their phone number and a one-time code sent by SMS.

From the portal, patients can:

  • View upcoming and past appointments.
  • See released lab results.
  • Check outstanding bills and payment history.
  • Submit feedback on their consultation.
Encourage patients to save the portal link on their phone's home screen for easy access.
Need help?

Contact your system administrator for account issues, access problems, or to report a bug. For clinical questions about using the system, refer to your departmental lead.
